Common Fence Repairs

We fix all types of fence damage across Frankston residential and commercial properties:

Post Repairs

  • Leaning or loose posts — Re-set posts in new concrete footings with proper depth and alignment
  • Rotted posts (base rot) — Cut out rotted timber base, sister new post section, or replace entire post
  • Concrete breakout — Remove failed concrete footings, excavate to 600–800mm, pour new footings with drainage
  • Storm-damaged posts — Emergency bracing and temporary repairs followed by permanent post replacement

Panel & Rail Repairs

  • Broken or missing palings — Replace individual palings to match existing fence; stain/paint to blend
  • Damaged Colorbond panels — Replace dented or corroded panels with matching color and profile
  • Broken rails — Sister new rails to existing or replace full rail sections with treated timber
  • Wind-damaged sections — Rebuild entire fence sections destroyed by storms; salvage existing posts where sound

Gate Repairs

  • Sagging gates — Re-hang with heavy-duty hinges, add diagonal bracing, adjust latch alignment
  • Broken latches & locks — Replace with quality stainless steel hardware; pool gates upgraded to AS1926.1 compliance
  • Gate post movement — Re-set gate posts in deeper footings with additional bracing
  • Self-closing mechanism faults — Replace or adjust pool gate closers to meet 10-second closing requirement

Coating & Finish Repairs

  • Peeling paint on timber — Strip, sand, prime, and repaint with Dulux Weathershield or equivalent
  • Rust staining on Colorbond — Remove rust, treat with inhibitor, touch-up with matching Colorbond paint
  • UV-damaged timber — Sand back grey weathered surface, apply penetrating oil or protective stain

Call 03 4152 7766 or request a callout online. We offer same-day site visits for emergency repairs (storm damage, safety hazards). For non-urgent repairs, we'll arrange a convenient assessment time within 48 hours.

Step 2: Damage Assessment & Quote

Local Considerations for Frankston

Coastal Corrosion

Frankston properties within 1km of Port Phillip Bay experience accelerated corrosion. We use 316 marine-grade stainless steel fasteners for all repairs near the beach (Frankston Beach, Seaford, Olivers Hill). Standard galvanized fixings will fail again within 12–24 months in salt-spray zones.

Wind Load

Strong southwesterly winds off the Bay cause frequent fence damage in exposed Frankston areas. For repeat failures, we recommend deeper post footings (800mm), braced corner posts, or switching to shadowline/slatted panels that allow wind to pass through.

Soil Movement

Sandy and reactive clay soils in Frankston cause post movement during wet seasons. We adjust footing depths (700–800mm) and use wider concrete bells to prevent recurring lean.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you repair my fence in Frankston?

We offer same-day callouts for emergency repairs (storm damage, safety hazards). Most non-urgent repairs are scheduled within 2–5 days and completed in 2–6 hours for simple jobs or 1–2 days for major reconstruction.

Can you match my existing fence for repairs?

Yes. We match timber species, paling profiles, Colorbond colors (all 22 standard colors), and post/rail dimensions. For older fences with discontinued materials, we recommend the closest modern equivalent and provide samples for approval.

Do you offer emergency fence repairs?

Yes. Call 03 4152 7766 for same-day emergency callouts. We prioritize safety hazards (collapsed fences blocking access, pool fences with gaps, gates off hinges). After-hours callouts may incur a $50 surcharge.

How do I know if I need repairs or full replacement?

If damage is isolated (1–3 posts, 1–2 panels, single gate), repair is usually most cost-effective. If 40%+ of the fence is damaged, posts are universally rotted, or the fence is 20+ years old with multiple failing sections, replacement often makes more financial sense. We'll recommend the best option during assessment.

Can you help with insurance claims for storm damage?

Yes. We provide written damage reports, photos, and itemized quotes suitable for home insurance claims. Most insurers accept our documentation without additional assessment. We can also liaise with loss adjusters on your behalf.

What's the difference between temporary and permanent repairs?

Temporary repairs (bracing, props, quick-fix fasteners) make the fence safe until permanent work can be scheduled—common after storms when we're booked out. Permanent repairs involve proper excavation, concrete footings, and full panel/post replacement. We always recommend permanent solutions unless you're planning full fence replacement soon.
