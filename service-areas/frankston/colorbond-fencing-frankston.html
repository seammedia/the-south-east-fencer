What We Do

Colorbond Profiles

  • Lapped panels — Standard overlap panels for full privacy; flat side faces your property, ribbed side faces neighbor
  • Good-neighbour panels — Both sides identical; posts enclosed between two sheets; ideal for shared boundaries
  • Shadowline panels — Horizontal slats with 10–20mm gaps for airflow and partial visibility; modern aesthetic
  • Mini-orb infill — Corrugated steel infill between timber or steel posts; classic rural/coastal look

Colorbond Colours

All 22 standard BlueScope Colorbond colours available. Most popular in Frankston:

  • Monument® — Charcoal grey; modern, neutral, hides dirt well
  • Basalt® — Deep grey-black; contemporary, matches dark window frames
  • Woodland Grey® — Mid-grey; versatile, complements most home styles
  • Surfmist® — Off-white cream; brightens gardens, stays cooler in sun
  • Evening Haze® — Soft grey-blue; coastal aesthetic, popular near the beach
  • Shale Grey™ — Warm mid-grey; timeless neutral

Standard Heights & Custom Options

  • 1.5m — Low privacy fence; allows views over top
  • 1.8m — Standard boundary fence height (most common)
  • 2.1m — Tall privacy fence; blocks second-story views
  • 2.4m+ — Custom heights (may require council approval)

Gates & Extras

  • Pedestrian gates (900mm–1.2m) — Matching Colorbond panels, self-closing hinges, quality latches
  • Double gates (2.4m–3.6m) — Vehicle access, driveway gates, farm gates
  • Slat extensions — Aluminium slat screens mounted on top of Colorbond panels for extra height/privacy
  • Lattice caps — Decorative lattice on top of solid panels (adds 300–600mm height)

Our Process in Frankston

Step 1: Free Quote & Site Visit

Call 03 4152 7766 or request a quote. We'll visit your property to measure boundaries, assess soil conditions, discuss profile (lapped/good-neighbour/shadowline), color options, gate positions, and council height limits. Most quotes emailed within 24 hours.

Step 2: Color & Profile Selection

Choose from 22 Colorbond colors using physical samples (we bring color chart to site visit). Select profile: lapped (most cost-effective), good-neighbour (both sides identical), or shadowline (modern, semi-private). We confirm all specs in writing before ordering materials.

Step 3: Council Compliance Check

We confirm Frankston City Council height limits (typically 2.0m rear/side, 1.2m front) and check for heritage overlays or corner block restrictions. For dividing fences, we can help serve notice to neighbors under Fences Act 1968 if cost-sharing is required.

Step 4: Installation

We:

  • Mark out post positions and remove old fencing (if applicable)
  • Dig post holes 600–800mm deep depending on fence height and soil type
  • Set galvanized steel posts in rapid-set concrete with laser-level alignment
  • Install top/bottom rails (C-section steel) once concrete cures (24 hours)
  • Attach Colorbond panels with marine-grade tek screws (coastal properties)
  • Hang gates with self-closing hinges and quality stainless steel latches
Typical install time: 1–3 days for standard residential fences (20–40m).

Step 5: Final Check & Warranty

We inspect plumb posts, level rails, secure fixings, and smooth gate operation. Old materials removed; site left clean. You'll receive BlueScope's 10-year perforation warranty documentation and our 5-year workmanship warranty.

Local Considerations for Frankston

Coastal Corrosion Resistance

Frankston properties near Port Phillip Bay require coastal-smart installation:

  • Marine-grade tek screws (not standard screws) for all panel fixing—prevents rust staining and panel blow-off
  • Galvanized steel posts (hot-dip galvanized after fabrication) for maximum corrosion resistance
  • BlueScope Colorbond steel with 10-year perforation warranty (only applies when installed to coastal specifications)
Properties within 1km of the beach are classified as "severe marine" environment by BlueScope—standard fixings will fail within 3–5 years.

Wind Loading

Frankston's exposure to strong southwesterly winds requires:

  • Deeper post footings (700–800mm) for fences over 1.8m or exposed sites
  • Additional mid-rails for tall fences (2.1m+) to prevent panel flex and noise
  • Braced corner posts for long fence runs (40m+) or high-wind coastal locations
Shadowline panels (with gaps) perform better in high-wind areas than solid lapped panels—allows wind to pass through.

Council Height Limits

Frankston City Council typically allows:

  • Rear and side boundaries: 2.0m without permit
  • Front boundaries: 1.2m maximum (must be 50% permeable—shadowline profile works, lapped doesn't)
  • Corner blocks: Sight-triangle rules restrict height to 1.2m within 9m of street intersection

Color Fade & Heat

Darker Colorbond colors (Monument, Basalt, Night Sky) absorb more heat and may feel hot to touch in summer. Lighter colors (Surfmist, Shale Grey, Dune) stay cooler but show dirt more readily. All Colorbond colors fade slightly over 10–15 years (more noticeable on darker shades). This is normal wear and doesn't affect structural integrity or warranty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Colorbond colors are most popular in Frankston?

Monument (charcoal grey) and Woodland Grey are most popular—modern, versatile, and hide dirt well. Coastal properties often choose lighter colors like Surfmist or Evening Haze which stay cooler and suit beachside aesthetics. We bring color samples to every site visit so you can see how colors look in your actual lighting.

How long does Colorbond fencing last?

Colorbond fencing lasts 30–50+ years in standard environments. Coastal properties (salt spray) may see 25–40 years if installed with marine-grade fixings. BlueScope provides a 10-year perforation warranty; the steel itself will last much longer. Posts and rails (galvanized steel) typically last 40–60 years.

Can I add Colorbond panels to my existing timber posts?

Only if existing timber posts are sound (no rot), properly spaced (2.4–2.7m centers), and solidly set in concrete. Most timber fences over 15 years old have rotted post bases or incorrect spacing—full replacement is usually more cost-effective. We assess existing fences during site visits and recommend the best option.

Do Colorbond fences make noise in the wind?

Lapped panels can "drum" or rattle in strong winds if not properly installed. We prevent this by:

  • Installing mid-rails on fences over 1.8m height
  • Using proper number of tek screws per panel (minimum 8 screws per 2.4m panel)
  • Ensuring panels are tight against rails with no flex
Shadowline panels (with gaps) don't drum because wind passes through. Good-neighbour panels (double-sided) are quieter than lapped panels.

Can pets damage Colorbond fences?

Dogs can't chew through Colorbond steel, but large dogs scratching/jumping can dent or scratch panels. We recommend:

  • Installing panels with flat side facing yard (less grippy for dogs)
  • Adding horizontal rail 300mm from ground (prevents dogs getting leverage)
  • Choosing darker colors (Monument, Basalt) that hide scratches better than light colors
Steel is far more dog-proof than timber which can be chewed, scratched through, or broken.

What's the difference between lapped and good-neighbour Colorbond?

Lapped panels show the flat side to one property and the ribbed/post side to the neighbor. They're most cost-effective. Good-neighbour panels have posts sandwiched between two sheets so both sides look identical—no visible posts. Good-neighbour costs 15–20% more but both neighbors get a "nice" side, which helps when cost-sharing under Fences Act.

Do I need marine-grade fixings in Frankston?

Yes—if your property is within 1km of Port Phillip Bay (Frankston Beach, Seaford beachfront, Olivers Hill), marine-grade 316 stainless tek screws are essential. Standard tek screws (zinc-plated or 304 stainless) will rust and stain panels within 2–3 years in salt-spray zones. The extra cost ($15–$25/m) is worth it for longevity.

Can I match an old Colorbond panel for repairs?

Yes, if the existing fence is less than 10 years old. Colorbond colors fade slightly over time, so new panels will look brighter initially but blend within 6–12 months. For fences over 15 years old, color matching is difficult—we may recommend painting old panels or full fence replacement. Discontinued colors (pre-2010) can't be matched exactly.

Do Colorbond fences need maintenance?

Minimal maintenance required. Hose down annually to remove dirt, salt, and debris. Check gate hinges/latches yearly and apply silicone spray if needed. Inspect for loose tek screws every 2–3 years (can back out over time in high-wind areas). No painting, staining, or sealing required—Colorbond's factory finish lasts the life of the fence.
