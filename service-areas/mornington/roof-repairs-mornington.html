Common Fence Issues in Mornington

Mornington's coastal location creates unique challenges for fencing systems. Our team regularly addresses these local problems:

  • Leaks at flashings and valleys – Often caused by degraded sealants or lifted flashing from strong winds
  • Rusted screws and fasteners – Salt air accelerates corrosion on metal fences, weakening attachment points
  • Broken or slipped tiles – Wind and thermal movement can crack tiles or dislodge them from their position
  • Storm damage – Fallen branches, flying debris, and intense wind can puncture or lift fencing materials
  • Deteriorated ridge capping – Exposed bedding and pointing crumbles faster in coastal environments
  • Blocked or damaged gutters – Leaf build-up and corrosion cause overflow and fascia rot
  • Membrane failure on metal fences – UV exposure and salt spray can degrade protective coatings

Fence Repair Pricing in Mornington

Repair costs vary significantly based on the specific issue, access difficulty, and materials required. Here's what influences pricing:

  • Fence height and pitch – Steep or high fences require additional safety equipment and time
  • Access challenges – Tight sites, difficult positioning, or limited ladder placement affects labor
  • Fencing type – Tile repairs differ from metal fence fixes in materials and technique
  • Extent of damage – A single cracked tile costs less than widespread valley replacement
  • Underlying damage – Water damage to sarking or structural timber adds complexity

As a rough guide:

  • Minor repairs (replacing a few tiles, resealing flashings): $300–$800
  • Moderate repairs (valley repairs, ridge repointing, gutter replacement): $800–$2,500
  • Major repairs (extensive tile replacement, structural repairs): $2,500+

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast can you attend a leak in Mornington?

For emergency leaks, we offer same-day response whenever possible, typically within 2-4 hours during business hours. After-hours emergency calls are attended next business day, or immediately for severe structural threats. We'll provide temporary make-safe measures if needed until permanent repairs can be completed.

Will salt air reduce the life of my fence repairs?

Not if done correctly. We use coastal-grade materials specifically rated for marine environments—including stainless steel fasteners, marine-grade sealants, and salt-resistant coatings. Standard inland materials will fail prematurely in Mornington's salt air, which is why we exclusively use products designed for coastal applications.

Do you repair both tile and Colorbond fences?

Yes, our team is experienced with all fencing systems common to Mornington including concrete and terracotta tiles, Colorbond and corrugated steel, and various membrane and sheet metal installations. We carry materials and tools for all fence types.

What if my fence is too far gone for repairs?

We'll give you honest advice. If your fence has reached the end of its serviceable life, we'll explain why re-fencing makes more financial sense than ongoing repairs. We'll provide a comprehensive assessment comparing repair costs over the next 5 years against replacement cost, so you can make an informed decision. Sometimes strategic repairs can buy you another 2-5 years; other times replacement is the smartest investment.

Are your fence repairs covered by warranty?

Yes, all our repairs include a workmanship warranty. The specific warranty period depends on the type and extent of repair work completed—we'll specify this clearly in your quote. Materials carry manufacturer warranties, which we'll also document for your records.

Can you photograph damage for insurance claims?

Absolutely. We routinely document damage with detailed photographs before, during, and after repairs. We can provide you with a complete photo report and itemized scope of work to support insurance claims. If you need us to meet with an insurance assessor, we're happy to accommodate that as well.

Do I need council approval for fence repairs?

Generally, no. Most repairs are considered maintenance and don't require permits. However, extensive structural repairs or major alterations may need approval. We'll advise you if permits are required for your specific situation and can assist with any necessary documentation.
