Services We Provide

1. New Fence Installations

Our Process: Quote to Completion

Step 1: Free On-Site Quote (30–45 min)

Call 03 4152 7766 or request a quote online. We'll schedule a site visit (usually within 2–3 business days). During the visit, we:

  • Measure the fence line
  • Discuss your style preferences, height, and material options
  • Check for slopes, boundaries, services (underground cables), and access
  • Provide a detailed written quote on the spot (or email within 24 hours)

No hidden fees. No pressure. Quote validity: 30 days.

Step 2: Design & Council (if required)

  • Standard fences (under 2.0m, no heritage overlay): No permit needed—we proceed to scheduling
  • Permit-required jobs: We prepare plans, lodge with council, and manage approval (4–8 weeks typical). Permit fees passed through at cost ($200–$800 depending on council).
  • Neighbour liaison: We can serve Fences Act notices on your behalf

Step 3: Scheduling & Materials

Once you accept the quote (or permit is approved), we:

  • Schedule installation (lead time: 1–3 weeks depending on season)
  • Order materials (Colorbond from BlueScope distributors, timber from local suppliers)
  • Call Dial Before You Dig to locate underground services

Step 4: Installation

Our licensed team arrives with all materials, tools, and equipment. Typical timelines:

  • 15m Colorbond fence: 1 day
  • 25m timber Hampton fence: 2 days (includes painting if pre-primed pickets supplied)
  • Pool fence (12m glass): 1 day
  • Repairs/extensions: Half-day to 1 day

We protect your lawn with drop sheets, work cleanly, and respect your property. Site supervisor checks quality before signing off.

Step 5: Cleanup & Final Inspection

  • Remove all off-cuts, packaging, and old fence materials (disposal included in quote)
  • Backfill post holes, level ground, re-seed lawn if disturbed
  • Walk you through the completed fence, answer questions
  • Issue tax invoice, provide warranty certificate (10 years workmanship, materials per manufacturer)

Step 6: Aftercare & Warranty

We offer a 10-year workmanship warranty on all new installations. Colorbond steel comes with BlueScope's 15-year perforation warranty (coastal <15km from ocean). If you experience any issues—posts shifting, panels rattling, gate alignment—call us. We stand by our work.

Materials & Coastal Durability

Melbourne's Eastern Suburbs and Mornington Peninsula face harsh coastal conditions—salt spray, high UV, and strong winds. We specify materials built to last:

Colorbond Steel

  • BlueScope Colorbond: Australian-made, Zincalume base with baked-on colour. 15-year perforation warranty (coastal <15km from ocean).
  • Thickness: 0.48mm BMT (base metal thickness)—heavier than cheap imported steel.
  • Posts: 50 × 50mm or 65 × 65mm RHS (rolled hollow section), hot-dip galvanised.
  • Fixings: Class 3 or 4 tek screws; stainless steel in exposed coastal sites (Sorrento, Rosebud beachfront).

Timber

  • Posts: H4-treated pine (ground contact rated), 90 × 90mm or 100 × 100mm.
  • Rails: H3.2 LOSP minimum; H4 for exposed horizontals.
  • Pickets/palings: H3-treated or dressed pine (primed before install).
  • Hardwood option: Spotted gum, merbau—naturally rot-resistant, can be oiled or painted.
  • Fixings: Hot-dip galvanised or 316 stainless nails/screws.

Glass (Pool Fencing)

  • Thickness: 12mm toughened (AS/NZS 2208 compliant).
  • Spigots: 316 marine-grade stainless (no rust in 20+ years).
  • Gates: Self-closing hinges (hydraulic soft-close), magnetic latches (AS1926 height requirements).

Why Coastal-Grade Materials Matter

We've seen $8,000 fences fail within 3 years due to cheap fixings or non-compliant timber treatments. A $40 upgrade to stainless screws extends fence life by 10+ years. In Sorrento, Rosebud, and Chelsea, salt spray corrodes standard zinc screws within 18 months—rust stains bleed through paint, posts loosen. We won't use them.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you provide a quote?

We aim for same-day or next-day site visits (Monday–Saturday). Quotes are usually provided on the spot, or emailed within 24 hours if we need to check material pricing. Rush quotes available for insurance/storm damage repairs.

Do you handle council permits?

Yes. If your fence requires a planning or building permit (heights over 2.0m, heritage overlays, corner blocks), we prepare plans, lodge applications, and liaise with council. Permit fees are passed through at cost. Approval typically takes 4–8 weeks depending on council workload.

What if my neighbour won't agree to the fence?

Under the Fences Act 1968, you can proceed with a boundary fence even if your neighbour disagrees, provided you serve a proper Notice of Intention 30 days prior. If they refuse to pay their 50%, you can build and recover costs later via Magistrates' Court or VCAT. We can guide you through the process or recommend a fencing lawyer if disputes escalate.

How long does a typical fence installation take?

15–20m Colorbond fence: 1 day. 25m timber Hampton fence: 2 days (3 if painting on-site). Glass pool fence: 1 day. Repairs and extensions: half-day to 1 day. Larger commercial jobs (50m+) may take 3–5 days. We confirm timelines in your quote.

Do you remove and dispose of old fences?

Yes, included in most quotes (or itemised separately if you prefer). We cut down old panels, extract posts, haul away timber/steel, and dispose legally (recycling where possible). Your property is left clean and ready for the new fence.

Can you fence on a slope or uneven ground?

Absolutely. We specialise in stepped or raked fences for sloping sites common across Mount Eliza and the Peninsula's hillside properties. Stepped panels maintain vertical lines; raked panels follow the slope. Both comply with council and look great. Expect a 15–30% cost premium for complex terrain.

What's your payment schedule?

Typical terms: 30% deposit on booking (secures scheduling, orders materials), 70% balance on completion. For larger jobs (>$10,000), we may split into thirds: deposit, mid-point (materials on-site), and completion. We don't require full payment upfront—that's a red flag in the industry.

Do you offer finance or payment plans?

Not directly, but we can recommend third-party providers like Humm, Brighte, or Zip who offer interest-free periods for home improvement. Apply before your quote to know your budget.

How do you handle underground services (water, gas, electrical)?

We call Dial Before You Dig (1100) at least 2 business days before starting. Service authorities mark underground lines with flags/paint. If services are in the way, we adjust post positions slightly (usually 100–200mm shift is enough) or hand-dig near marked areas. Safety first—we've never hit a service line in 15+ years.

What if I'm not home during installation?

No problem, provided we have clear site access (unlocked gates, no dogs loose). We'll confirm timing the day before, take progress photos, and call you when complete for final inspection. Many clients are at work during our installs—we're trustworthy, insured, and your property is respected.
