What drives fence replacement costs in Australia?

Peninsula fencing costs depend on five key factors:

  • Total fence area: Measured including pitch and overhangs—typically 1.1–1.4× your floor area
  • Material and profile: Colorbond steel vs concrete/terracotta tiles, with profile affecting labour time
  • Pitch and complexity: Steep fences and multi-valley designs require more safety equipment and skilled labour
  • Access and storeys: Difficult sites with narrow access or double-storey heights increase setup costs
  • Scope additions: Insulation, sarking, skylights, and new gutters significantly impact total cost

Colorbond vs tiles: Peninsula pricing realities

Material choice affects both supply and installation costs on the Peninsula:

Colorbond steel advantages

  • Faster installation: Lighter sheets mean quicker progress, reducing labour costs
  • Lower structural load: Existing frames rarely need strengthening
  • Coastal performance: COLORBOND Ultra resists salt air corrosion better than standard steel
  • Easier removal: Less disposal weight and time compared to tiles

Tile considerations

  • Higher material cost: Concrete and terracotta tiles carry premium pricing
  • Slower installation: Individual tile placement takes more time than sheet fencing
  • Weight factors: May require structural assessment or beam upgrades
  • Coastal durability: Salt spray can cause tile surface degradation over time

Peninsula conditions affecting your fence choice

Local weather patterns influence both performance and maintenance costs:

Coastal exposure

Properties within 1km of Port Phillip Bay face increased salt air exposure. COLORBOND Ultra performs better than standard steel in these conditions, while tiles may show surface erosion over 15–20 years.

Wind and weather

Peninsula southerlies and storm activity favour well-fastened metal fencing. Our installations exceed minimum fixing requirements for improved wind resistance.

Thermal performance

Both Colorbond and tiles benefit from proper sarking and insulation. The calculator includes sarking and bulk insulation options that significantly improve comfort and energy efficiency.

When restoration beats replacement

Not every fence needs full replacement. Consider fence restoration if:

  • Structure and sarking are sound (under 25 years old)
  • Only surface coating or minor repairs are needed
  • Budget constraints make staged works preferable
  • Council approval timeframes favour restoration scope

Our Peninsula specialists can assess whether restoration or replacement offers better value for your specific situation.

Fence replacement cost – FAQs

What's included in the calculator?

Supply & install of the chosen fencing, standard flashings, fixings, and any options you select such as sarking or gutters. Structural works and asbestos are excluded.

How accurate is the estimate?

It's a planning-level guide (±10–20%). We'll confirm after site inspection.

Colorbond or tiles — which costs more?

Material rates differ by region and profile. Colorbond is often lighter and faster to install; tiles can carry higher material cost and removal.
