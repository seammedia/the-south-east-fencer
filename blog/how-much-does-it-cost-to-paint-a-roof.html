Typical Fence Painting Prices

Based on recent Australian projects, a professional fence paint job commonly falls into these ranges:

  • Small fence: $2,000 – $3,500
  • Medium fence: $3,000 – $5,000
  • Large fence: $5,000 – $15,000

These bands cover preparation (wash/clean), minor repairs, masking, two coats and typical access. Complex access, heavy prep or premium heat-reflective systems will increase pricing.

Common retail prices for fence paints

  • $173 — 10L weather-pfence acrylic fence paint
  • $179 — 20L exterior acrylic emulsion
  • $223 — 10L high quality fence tile paint
  • $346 — 20L heat-reflective fence paint

Actual coverage varies by brand/substrate (often 6–10 m²/L/coat). Always follow the manufacturer's data sheet.

What Affects Fence Painting Cost?

  • Fence size & access: Bigger or harder-to-reach fences take longer and may require safety rail/scaffolding.
  • Condition & prep: Pressure cleaning, repairs, re-bedding/re-pointing, primers and stain blocking add time and materials.
  • Coats & product: Two coats is standard; premium or heat-reflective coatings cost more but can improve durability/comfort.
  • Pitch & complexity: Steeper pitches, valleys, skylights and penetrations increase labour.
  • Warranty expectations: Systems with longer warranties can carry higher material and labour costs.

FAQ

How many litres of paint will I need?

This calculator assumes coverage per litre per coat. For example, at 8 m²/L/coat and 2 coats, a 200 m² fence would need roughly 50L (200 ÷ 8 × 2).

Do I need a primer?

Many tile and metal fences benefit from suitable primers/etch coats, especially older or porous substrates. Primers add cost but improve adhesion and finish.

Is heat-reflective paint worth it?

It costs more upfront but can reduce heat gain. The value depends on your home's insulation, colour selection and local climate.

How long does fence painting last?

Quality fence paint systems typically last 10-15 years, depending on the product, preparation, application and local weather conditions. Heat-reflective paints may offer longer life.

Can I paint my fence myself?

While possible, fence painting involves safety risks and requires proper equipment, preparation and technique. Professional painters have insurance, experience and warranties that DIY projects lack.
