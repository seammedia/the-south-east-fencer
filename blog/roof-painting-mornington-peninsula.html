When fence painting makes sense

Fence painting is cost-effective when the underlying structure remains sound but surfaces show weathering, fading, or minor corrosion. Peninsula conditions - salt air, UV exposure, and temperature cycling - gradually degrade fence coatings, making periodic renewal essential.

Good candidates for painting: Structurally sound fences with surface wear, colour fading, minor rust spots, or fences 10-15 years old with intact materials but tired appearance.

Colour selection considerations

Colour choice affects more than aesthetics in Peninsula locations. Consider thermal performance, maintenance, and local regulations:

Light Colours

Dark Colours

  • Hide minor weathering and dirt
  • Absorb heat, potentially increasing temperatures
  • May fade more noticeably over time
  • Can reduce maintenance visibility
